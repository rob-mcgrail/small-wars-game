# HQs

At the moment orders come from thin air. There should be two layers of HQ units. HQ units are regular units (let's make them toyotas without mounted guns for now). But they have the HQ property. 

To make this work, also every unit has optics, which decides their line of site distance (modelled like weapons), or existing fighting units have very generic (eyesight?) optics.

But our HQ vehicles have something that sees further, but seems plausible for a sophisticatedish militia to posses. Potentially just binocs? That gives them some realistically enhanced line of site.

So they have more line of site, and then HQ vehicles also have an effective communication range. This is shown as a ring along with their weapon ranges. Their communication device is modelled like optics and weapons. Decide on something realistic, like a radio system.

There are two layers of HQ. Name these accordingly, but basically on our map add a top level HQ, and then have 1 HQ command unit underneath it.

THe unit panel for all units shows what HQ unit they are directly under. Or it says "Out of communications" and the unit can not receive new orders.

If an HQ unit is out of range of its parent HQ unit, units under it can receive orders, but the parent HQ unit can not move itself? Or is that too restrictive.

Units under an HQ unit receive a buff to their ability to process orders. Units in LoS to an HQ unit receive an even bigger buff, and minor buffs to morale and accuracy and a minor resistance to supression.

These also apply to HQ units in LoS of their parent HQ unit.

Changing parent HQ unit has a cost (let's say, a baseline of 15 minutes where the unit will say "Switching HQ"). This applies when re-entering the communications range of your HQ or when leaving one range and entering another.
